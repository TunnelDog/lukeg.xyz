Unfortunate Coincidence
By the time you swear you’re his,
Shivering and sighing,
And he vows his passion is
Infinite, undying–
Lady, make a note of this:
One of you is lying.
Dorothy Parker, 1893
Uncertainty
A test composed by my sluggishness,
Monitored by indulgence.
Multiple choices follow each question,
Each seeming less correct than the last.

Must I eliminate the false before it eliminates me?
And if I am eliminated,
I will not deject myself—
The choice was likely wrong to begin with.
Luke Gamertsfelder; October 29, 2024
Planning Ahead
My week at a glance,
Convenient on a calendar.
I never scheduled my playdates,
Was marked complete at the end of the slide.

Maintaining the cumbersome conformity,
Reaching back to youth,
Let’s all follow suit, and
Add a randomize button!
Luke Gamertsfelder; September 23, 2024
Separation
Your absence has gone through me
Like thread through a needle.
Everything I do is stitched with its color.
W. S. Merwin; Downtown 6 Train
Timeless Intentions Haiku
Let us build today,
On tomorrow's intentions,
Embracing our fate.
Luke Gamertsfelder; May 29, 2024
The Underachiever
Humanity depletes its natural resource,
As the children wind up their toys.
Marmots disregard their past treasures, while
The idle man waits for his interview.
Luke Gamertsfelder; March 2, 2024
Fireproof
My candles can’t burn down
Any more.
They can’t burn anything down,
These days.
They just flicker with a false incandescence
Less and less…
Dimmer and dimmer…
As their batteries discharge.

I only replace them,
The batteries, I mean,
When you visit.
So between your visits,
Each night is dimmer and dimmer.
Until I am alone in the dark.
Joe Hills; July 18, 2022 joehills.net
